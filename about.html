About Florida Birds Daily

Florida Birds Daily is a small personal hobby project — one species, one photo, one place, every morning. Florida is loud with birds: roseate spoonbills slow-stepping through Merritt Island, anhingas drying their wings on every cypress branch, scrub-jays that exist nowhere else on earth. The goal here is to spend a minute a day with one of them.

The site is built on a simple automation. Every day at 8 AM Eastern, a script picks a Florida bird from recent eBird reports, pulls a creative-commons photo from Wikimedia Commons, lifts a short bio from Wikipedia, and (when one exists) links a recording from xeno-canto. The result is a daily page and a feed entry, published to GitHub Pages with no humans in the loop.

That means the bios are encyclopedic, not personal — I haven't seen most of these birds. It also means the photos belong to the photographers who shared them, and the sightings belong to the eBirders who logged them. Where you see a face in a photo or a hotspot pin, those are real people doing real fieldwork. Credit is on every page; if anything is mis-attributed, I want to fix it — reach out and I will.

What's automated, what isn't

Automated: the daily fetch from eBird, photo and bio lookup, page generation, manifest rebuild, RSS, sitemap, and deploy. Not automated: editorial judgment. If a script picks a poor photo or a bio reads strangely, that's a bug, not a person. Issues and corrections welcome.
